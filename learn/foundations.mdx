title: Foundations
description: Core ideas, vocabulary, and workflow for responsible red team learning.
---

Red team learning starts with clear scope, careful notes, and repeatable practice. Before running tools, make sure you know what system you are allowed to test, what behavior is out of scope, and how you will record what you learn.

## Core Workflow

1. Define the target and authorization.
2. Gather only the information needed for the exercise.
3. Form a small hypothesis.
4. Test it in the lab.
5. Document the result, command, evidence, and next step.

## Vocabulary

- **Asset**: A system, application, account, or service in scope.
- **Scope**: The exact boundary of what you are allowed to test.
- **Finding**: A reproducible weakness, misconfiguration, or behavior worth reporting.
- **Impact**: What the finding could allow in the approved environment.
- **Remediation**: The change that reduces or removes the risk.

## Habits

Keep your notes organized by target, timestamp, command, result, and interpretation. Good notes are what turn a one-time solve into a reusable skill.
