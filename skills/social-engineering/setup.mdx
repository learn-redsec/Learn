Technical vulnerabilities are not always the entry point. In many real-world red team engagements, the most effective initial access vector is a human one. Social engineering exploits trust, urgency, and habit rather than software bugs. This course teaches the concepts, tools, and ethics of OSINT and social engineering for authorized engagements.

## What You Will Learn

- Gather information about targets from publicly available sources (OSINT).
- Build target profiles that inform attack scenarios.
- Understand how phishing campaigns are constructed and detected.
- Apply pretexting frameworks in authorized exercises.
- Evaluate and document social engineering findings.

## Ethics and Authorization

Social engineering has a higher potential for psychological harm than technical exploitation. Before any exercise:

- Get **explicit written authorization** that covers the social engineering techniques you plan to use.
- Know who the target population is and who is **explicitly excluded** (e.g., employees on medical leave, contractors).
- Agree on a **safe word** or reporting mechanism so that employees who feel genuinely uncomfortable can stop the exercise.
- Debrief after the exercise and treat everyone with respect—the goal is organizational improvement, not embarrassment.

All exercises in this course should be practiced on yourself, your own accounts, and authorized targets only.

## Setup

```sh
# Install OSINT tools
sudo apt install -y theharvester recon-ng whois dnsutils

# Install GoPhish
wget https://github.com/gophish/gophish/releases/download/v0.12.1/gophish-v0.12.1-linux-64bit.zip
unzip gophish-v0.12.1-linux-64bit.zip
chmod +x gophish
./gophish
```

Create accounts for:
- [Shodan](https://shodan.io) (free tier available)
- [Hunter.io](https://hunter.io) (email finder)
- [LinkedIn](https://linkedin.com) (employee enumeration)
